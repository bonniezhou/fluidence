Fluidence

by Bonnie Zhou

Fluidence is a display typeface that captures the expressive flow of Chinese brush calligraphy in Latin letters. With its dynamic modulation, bold contrast, and abrupt directional shifts, it evokes the energy of an ink brush moving vividly to the whimsical temperament of its holder. A playful and modern typeface, Fluidence nods to the rich, timeless beauty of Chinese and East Asian culture. Fluidence is best suited for headlines, branding, posters, or any project that needs a touch of movement and charm.

The Inspiration: Why Chinese brush calligraphy?

For the past few years, I have been studying Chinese calligraphy as a way to reconnect with my ancestral culture. What captivates me most about the art form is its ability to embody both grace and vitality with each brush stroke.

The specific calligraphy piece that I’ve been studying is called 蘭亭序 (Lanting Xu), or Orchid Pavilion Preface, by 王羲之 (Wang Xizhi). Widely considered the greatest piece of calligraphy ever created, it serves as the basis for this typeface.

Some things I wanted to capture in Fluidence are the feeling of flow, a smooth, carefree energy with a bit of whimsicality. The rhymthic movement creating a strong contrast and hairline connections between strokes. Mostly round shapes that change direction with abrupt angles and pointy terminals.

Note: It’s important to mention here what I didn’t want my typeface to look like. I didn't want it to look like so-called “chop suey fonts” which were created during an era when Orientalism surged in America and now read as extremely stereotypical, if not downright racist. What I wanted was a typeface that represented my Chinese background and my love of calligraphy that my fellow East Asian diaspora could feel connected to and proud of.

What's a variable font?

A variable font is a single font file that contains multiple variations (width, weight, optical size, etc.) of a typeface. You can set the value of these variations using CSS. For example, the variable font 'Fluidence VF' contains all the weight variations of Fluidence, from Regular to Black. Here, weight is called an axis that can be set within an allowable range defined in the typeface. Using CSS, you can set Fluidence to have any weight within its defined range, from 400 (Regular) to 900 (Black).

In contrast, a static font represents only one specific style of the typeface. Before variable font technology, typefaces were produced as separate individual files, where each file would represent one specific width/weight/style combination. For example 'Fluidence Regular', 'Fluidence Bold', and 'Fluidence Black' would each be a separate file. This means that if you have a large font with many styles, you could end up with 20 or 30 different font files to represent a complete typeface.

Fluidence is available as individual static fonts ('Fluidence Regular', 'Fluidence Bold', and 'Fluidence Black') and also as a variable font with the complete weight axis ('Fluidence VF'). If you purchase the variable font, you also get the 3 static fonts as a package for free, and vice versa.

Which license do I need?

Fonts can be used in several ways, the main ones being:

  • print/desktop use: installing the font onto a device for usage in creative applications like Adobe Illustrator, Figma, etc. (to create designs for print materials, graphics, logos, videos, etc.)
  • web use: embedding the font on your website, and displaying it using CSS
  • app use: embedding the font in a software app, where users can view but not edit with or download the font

My fonts come with a single tiered license that covers all of these uses, instead of having a different license for each type of usage. Just choose the tier that reflects the scale of your use, and that’s it. Your license is a one-time purchase with no expiration date. If your needs grow later, you can upgrade to a higher tier by just paying the difference:

  • Basic License — for use on up to 2 devices for print/desktop, up to 20K monthly page views for web
  • Small License — for use on up to 5 devices for print/desktop, up to 100K monthly page views for web
  • Medium License — for use on up to 15 devices for print/desktop, up to 500K monthly page views for web, 1 app embed
  • Large License — for use on up to 30 devices for print/desktop, up to 1M monthly page views for web, 1 app embed

If you're working solo or have a small operation, the Basic License is a great starting point. As your business or project expands, you can easily upgrade to a higher tier license to match your growing needs.

About the Designer

Bonnie Zhou is a Canadian artist and designer based in Brooklyn, New York. Her work explores the visual beauty of language, emphasizing expressiveness, fluidity, and rhythm. Her practice includes Chinese brush calligraphy, neon glass bending, figure painting, and type design — all rooted in same principles of flow and movement. In a previous life, Bonnie worked as a frontend engineer on Google's Material Design system, where she took her very first type design workshop.

Bonnie holds a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from the University of Waterloo, and a Postgraduate Certificate in Type Design from Type West by Letterform Archive. Fluidence was initialized as her final project during her time in the Type West Class of 2024.
